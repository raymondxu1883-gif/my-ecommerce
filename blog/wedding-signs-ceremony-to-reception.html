Wedding Signs Guide: From Ceremony to Reception

February 26, 2026

Comprehensive wedding signage ensures your guests feel welcomed and informed throughout your celebration. This guide covers every sign you needâ€”from the moment guests arrive until the last dance.

Why Wedding Signs Are Essential

Proper signage accomplishes multiple goals:

  • Guides guests through venues smoothly
  • Reduces stress for wedding party and planners
  • Creates beautiful photo opportunities
  • Shows attention to detail guests appreciate
  • Maintains event flow seamlessly

Studies show weddings with comprehensive signage have 45% fewer guest complaints about finding locations.

Ceremony Signs

1. Welcome Sign (Ceremony)

Purpose: Officially welcome guests to your wedding

  • "Welcome to [Your Names]"
  • Wedding date
  • Venue name (optional)

2. Parking/Valet Sign

Purpose: Direct guests to parking areas

3. Unplugged Ceremony Sign

Purpose: Request guests put away phones

  • "Unplugged Ceremony"
  • "Please Silence Your Phones"
  • "Be in the Moment"

Reception Signs

1. Reception Welcome Sign

Purpose: Welcome guests to reception

  • "Welcome to the Reception"
  • Dinner/dancing starts at [time]
  • Brief venue directions

2. Seating Chart

Purpose: Help guests find table assignments

  • Acrylic mirror seating chart
  • Framed seating chart
  • Mirror with florals

3. Table Numbers

Purpose: Guide guests to specific tables

4. Bar Signs

Purpose: Direct guests to bar areas

  • "Open Bar" / "Cash Bar"
  • Signature drink names

5. Food Station Signs

Purpose: Label food areas

  • Buffet signs
  • Dessert table signs
  • Cake cutting sign

Sign Placement Timeline

Before Guests Arrive (1-2 hours)

  1. Parking signs
  2. Welcome signs
  3. Directionals
  4. Seating charts
  5. Table numbers

Before Reception Starts

  1. Menu cards
  2. Bar signs
  3. Food station signs
  4. Guestbook sign
