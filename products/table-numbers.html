Frequently Asked Questions

How many table numbers do I need?

You'll need one table number for each table at your reception. Most weddings with 10-20 tables use table numbers. For larger events, consider a seating chart instead.

Do I need table numbers or a seating chart?

Table numbers work well for formal dinners with assigned tables. If you have assigned seats within tables (name place cards), a seating chart showing table assignments is recommended.

Can I see a proof before ordering?

Yes! After you submit your quote request, we'll create a digital proof showing your numbers in your chosen style and color. You can request changes until you love it.

Do the table numbers stand on their own?

Yes, our acrylic table numbers have a built-in free standing base. They sit perfectly on table centerpieces without needing additional holders.

What size should I get?

For round tables (60-72"), we recommend 4"x6" or 5"x7". For larger tables or long rectangular tables, 6"x9" provides better visibility.

📝 Buyer's Guide: Wedding Table Numbers

Why Do You Need Table Numbers?

Table numbers help guests find their seats quickly and efficiently during your reception. They also add to your table decor and create a more organized, professional look.

Table Numbers vs. Seating Charts

Table Numbers10-20 tables, buffet or plated dinnerSimple table assignments
Seating ChartsLarge weddings, assigned seatingGuests need table & seat info
